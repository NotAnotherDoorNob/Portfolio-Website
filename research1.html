Exploring the Ideal Grocery Shopping Experience

This project was a graduate collaberation in which we researched consumers feelings and experiences with grocery shoping. Together, we learned how to conduct a variety of research techniques, including qualitative surveys and collaboration with stakeholders through co-design sessions.


Research Methods

Qualtrics Survey

We sent out an online survey inquiring about people's grocery shopping habbits and attitudes toward the experience. Approximately 120 people responed

Journey Map

Participants were asked to make a map of their average grocery shopping experience and explain it to the group.

Co-Design

Participants were separated into groups and given tools to build a model of their ideal store. They were then asked to explain their creation to the other groups.

Journey Mapping

The journey mapping aspect of this research session involved participants making a chronological map of their average grocery shopping experience with a variety of preselected images relating to grocery. Afterwards, participants were asked to describe their map to the group, wich was video recorded and analyzed post session. The maps were also anaylized and coded for meaning and patterns.

Some noteable Common Behavior:

  • Participants frequently brought their own bags
  • Participants made lists before going to the store, but rarely stuck to the list exactly.
  • Participants avoided crowds and noted disliking the checkout process
  • Participants spent the most time talking about the checkout process

Insights:

  • There is a need for stores to re-evaluate the user experience in the checkout process
  • There is a need and oppertunity plastic bags from the system.
  • Customers are crowd averse and do not like to socialize while shopping

Co-design Session: Build your Ideal Store

In thiss session, were given building materials and asked to create a model of their ideal grocery store. This allowed us to analyize problem areas of the grocery shopping experience and generate potential solutions based on their creations. After the participants finished their models, they were asked to present their store infrony of the other group. These presentations were recorded and coded for insights.

Insights for a more open concept

  • There is a wont for wider, more accessable isles
  • Ability to manuver without getting stuck
  • More information about available products

Insights for faster, more efficient checkout

  • Smart carts and personal scanners
  • New designs for carts and checkout
