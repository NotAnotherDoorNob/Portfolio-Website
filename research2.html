Exploring the Ideal Grocery Shopping Experience

This project was a graduate collaberation in which we researched consumers feelings and experiences with grocery shoping. Together, we learned how to conduct a variety of research techniques, including qualitative surveys and collaboration with stakeholders through co-design sessions.






Work Shop #1: Verbal Interviews

The initial workshop involved verbal interveiws of hospital staff and former patients. During interviews post-it notes with insights were posted and organized into pre-established catagories.




The information on the post-its were then organized into larger subject clusters.




Work Shop #2:Patient Room Co-Design

In the second workshop, participants were asked questiions and prompted to articulate their ideas by drawing and interacting with room blueprints. This format allowed participants to engage spatially and to think procedurally about problems in their workflow. The questions asked were based on the catagories from workshop #1. Notes were also taken and organized on posterboard.







Work Shop #3:Online Survey

Because of the impact of COVID-19, our group had to pivot away from doing in person interviews towards methods which functioned online and remotely. For our third workshop, we created a comprehensive survey on Qualtrics. At this stage we began to narrow down our insights into potential design solutions. We used this survet to get feedback on potential ideas.






Final Design Insights and Suggestions

We took our highest meta-ranked ideas an mocked them up into illustration. Each idea was sorted based on scope and feasability.








Workshop #4: Breif follow-Up

After finalizing our design, we reached out to a few of the hospital staff in order to see if our insights have remained relevant in light of the COVID-19 outbreak.
